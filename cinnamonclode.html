What is True Cinnamon?

True Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum) is a culinary and medicinal treasure that originates exclusively from Sri Lanka. Unlike its more common counterpart, Cassia cinnamon, Ceylon cinnamon is distinguished by its delicate taste, lighter color, and thin, papery bark.

Ceylon Cinnamon

  • Light brown color
  • Multiple thin layers that can be easily ground
  • Sweet, delicate flavor with floral notes
  • Low coumarin content (safe for regular consumption)
  • Premium price reflecting its quality

Cassia Cinnamon

  • Dark reddish-brown color
  • Single thick, hard layer
  • Strong, spicy flavor with a bitter aftertaste
  • High coumarin content (potentially harmful in large amounts)
  • More affordable and widely available

Origin & Uniqueness

The story of Ceylon cinnamon is intrinsically tied to Sri Lanka's rich history and landscape. For centuries, this island nation has been the world's primary source of true cinnamon, with traditions passed down through generations of skilled craftsmen.

The cinnamon peelers of Sri Lanka, known as "Salagama," are masters of their craft, carefully harvesting and processing the inner bark of the cinnamon tree to create the perfect quills. This meticulous process involves:

  • Harvesting young shoots during the rainy season when the bark is most pliable
  • Careful removal of the outer bark to expose the precious inner layer
  • Allowing the bark to ferment for 24 hours before gently removing it
  • Rolling multiple thin layers into the characteristic "quills"
  • Natural drying in the sun, where the quills curl into their telltale shape

This traditional process, combined with Sri Lanka's unique growing conditions, creates a spice that is truly irreplaceable in the culinary world.

Culinary Uses

With its sweet, delicate flavor profile, Ceylon cinnamon elevates both sweet and savory dishes around the world.

Ceylon Cinnamon Tea

A warming beverage that combines the subtle sweetness of Ceylon cinnamon with black tea and a touch of honey.

Sri Lankan Curry

Authentic Sri Lankan curries use Ceylon cinnamon as a key spice, creating depth and complexity without overpowering other flavors.

Classic Cinnamon Rolls

Traditional cinnamon rolls reach new heights when made with genuine Ceylon cinnamon's complex flavor profile.

How to Identify True Ceylon Cinnamon

Look for these key characteristics when purchasing:

  • Light tan color (rather than reddish-brown)
  • Multiple thin layers that are easily breakable
  • Sweet, subtle aroma without strong spiciness
  • Labeled as "Ceylon Cinnamon" or "Cinnamomum verum"
