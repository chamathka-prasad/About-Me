Our Story

From Sri Lanka to the World

KW INDUSTRIES was founded with a vision to share Sri Lanka's finest cinnamon with the world. What began as a small family operation in the cinnamon-rich regions of Sri Lanka has grown into a respected exporter of premium Ceylon cinnamon.

For generations, our family has cultivated and processed cinnamon using traditional methods combined with modern quality control techniques. This unique blend of heritage and innovation allows us to deliver cinnamon products of exceptional quality.

Today, we supply bulk cinnamon to importers, wholesalers, and food manufacturers across North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia, while maintaining our commitment to quality, sustainability, and fair trade practices.

Ceylon Cinnamon vs. Cassia

Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum) is distinctly different from Cassia cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) in several important ways:

Characteristic Ceylon Cinnamon Cassia Cinnamon
Scientific Name Cinnamomum zeylanicum Cinnamomum cassia
Origin Sri Lanka China, Indonesia, Vietnam
Appearance Thin, fragile layers; light brown Thick, hard bark; dark reddish-brown
Taste Delicate, sweet, complex Strong, pungent, slightly bitter
Coumarin Content 0.004% (very low) Up to 5% (potentially harmful)
Price Higher (premium product) Lower (common in supermarkets)

Our commitment to quality ensures you receive authentic Ceylon cinnamon with all its natural benefits and none of the potential risks associated with Cassia varieties.

Import Regulations by Market

United States

FDA regulated. Requires prior notice for food imports. Ceylon cinnamon generally recognized as safe (GRAS). Must comply with FDA labeling requirements.

Key Documents: FDA Prior Notice, Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Phytosanitary Certificate

European Union

Strict EU food safety regulations apply. Must meet maximum residue levels (MRLs) for pesticides. Coumarin content restrictions apply (recommended limit 0.1 mg/kg body weight).

Key Documents: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Phytosanitary Certificate, Health Certificate

Middle East

Halal certification preferred but not mandatory for cinnamon. GCC Standardization Organization (GSO) standards apply in Gulf countries.

Key Documents: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Certificate of Origin, Halal Certificate (if applicable)

Japan

Strict pesticide regulations. Positive list system for agricultural chemical residues. Must comply with Japanese labeling requirements.

Key Documents: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Phytosanitary Certificate, Ingredient List

Important Note

Import regulations are subject to change. We recommend consulting with your local customs broker or food import authority for the most current requirements. Our team can assist with providing any additional documentation required by your market.
